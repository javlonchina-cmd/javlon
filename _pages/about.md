Welcome!

I am an MSc student in Experimental Nuclear and Particle Physics at the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS), based at the Institute of High Energy Physics (IHEP) in Beijing, China. My research interests center on high energy physics experiments, precise measurements of Standard Model physics, Higgs physics, and searches for physics Beyond the Standard Model (BSM).

I completed my Bachelor's degree in Physics at the National University of Uzbekistan (2020–2024) in Tashkent. During my undergraduate studies, I gained hands-on experience in experimental nuclear and high energy physics through internships and laboratory work, including processing photonuclear emulsions and bubble chamber data at the Physical-Technical Institute of the Academy of Sciences of Uzbekistan.

I have participated in several prestigious international research programmes at the **Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR)**, Dubna, Russia. As a **START Programme intern** (2023), I worked on measurements of alpha-particle ranges in the fragmentation of ¹²C and ¹⁶O nuclei with relativistic hadrons using nuclear track emulsions. As an **INTEREST Programme intern** (2023), I investigated the production and spectroscopy of new neutron-rich isotopes near the N = 126 shell closure using multinucleon transfer reactions.

In 2026, I participated in the **CMS Data Analysis School (CMSDAS)** at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), where I trained in CMS software and physics analysis techniques relevant to the Large Hadron Collider experiments.

Prior to my master's studies, I worked as a Physics Laboratory Assistant in the Department of Nuclear Physics and Astronomy at Samarkand State University, where I supported undergraduate students in nuclear and experimental physics laboratory work.

I am the co-author of two publications in the Bulletin of Samarkand State University on relativistic heavy-ion collision physics, covering the analysis of particle spectra in Cu+Cu collisions and central dC-, CC-, and CTa-collisions.
