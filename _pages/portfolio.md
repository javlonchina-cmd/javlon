## 🔬 Research Overview

My research interests lie at the heart of experimental high energy physics: precise tests of the Standard Model, Higgs boson physics, and searches for new physics Beyond the Standard Model (BSM). My academic path has combined nuclear emulsion techniques, heavy-ion collision data analysis, and modern collider physics tools such as the CMS experiment software framework.

---

## ⚛️ High Energy Physics & CMS Experiment

As a master's student at IHEP/UCAS, I am developing expertise in collider physics analysis techniques:

- Trained at the **CMS Data Analysis School (CMSDAS)** at USTC, Hefei (2026)
- CMS software framework and physics object reconstruction
- Practical exercises in High Energy Physics data analysis relevant to LHC experiments

---

## 💥 Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collision Physics

My early research focused on particle production in relativistic nuclear collisions:

- Analysis of pion, kaon, proton, and antiproton spectra in Cu+Cu collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Comparative study of central dC-, CC-, and CTa-collisions at 4.2 AGeV/c momentum
- Statistical fitting methods for particle spectra in heavy-ion collisions

**Publications:** Bulletin of Samarkand State University (2023, 2024)

---

## 🧪 Nuclear Emulsion & Bubble Chamber Techniques

Experimental nuclear physics methods learned during internships:

- **JINR START Programme:** Measurement of alpha-particle ranges in fragmentation of ¹²C and ¹⁶O nuclei using nuclear track emulsions
- **JINR INTEREST Programme:** Production and spectroscopy of neutron-rich isotopes near the N = 126 shell closure via multinucleon transfer reactions
- Processing of photonuclear emulsions and bubble chamber data at the Physical-Technical Institute of Uzbekistan
